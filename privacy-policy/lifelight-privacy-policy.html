Introduction

We regard your privacy and the handling of your personal data with the utmost importance. This Privacy Notice details how we collect, use and securely store any personal data submitted to us through use of our website (https://lifelight.ai) and the Lifelight application and services provided as part of the application (‘Lifelight Application’). Separate privacy policies will apply to clinical trials in accordance with the respective trial protocol.

Our roles when handling personal data

We are what is known as a ‘data controller’ for processing your personal data submitted to us through our website.

For patient or user data submitted to us through use of our Lifelight Application, Xim is what is known as the ‘data processor’ and not the ‘data controller’ other than where Xim is required to store your data to comply with its regulatory obligations in which case Xim is a ‘data controller’.

The data controller has the primary role in relation to decisions concerning your personal data, such as when you exercise any rights available to you under data protection laws, though we (as a data processor) also have responsibilities in relation to the security of your personal data. Where we are acting as a ‘data processor’, we will be sharing your personal data with the ‘data controller’ such as your healthcare provider, intermediaries providing services to your healthcare provider or service provider who made the Lifelight Application available to you as part of their own product.

What types of information do we collect?

Personal data means any information about an individual from which that person can be identified. We will never collect any unnecessary personal data from you and do not process your information in any way, other than as specified in this notice.

We may collect, use, store and transfer different kinds of personal data about you which may include:

  • Name
  • Job title
  • Email address (personal / business)
  • Organisation details
  • Telephone Numbers
  • Vital Signs and Related Data meaning heart rate / pulse, blood pressure and respiration, as well as biometrics (height, age and sex), pixel values, any in/output into the Lifelight Application and measurement ID
  • Technical information from usage of the Lifelight Application and our website (including IP address, device ID, operating system and platform and other technology on the devices you use to access)
  • Unique device ID issued by Xim
  • Usage information which includes information about how you interact with or use our website and Lifelight App
  • Preferences about receiving marketing from us

We DO NOT collect:

The following types of data from your device:

  • Still images
  • Video
  • Audio / sound

However, when you use the Lifelight Application we do upload data from your device which we use to predict your vital signs. This data contains only signals generated from light reflected by specific regions of your face during the time taken for the measurement, as well as your biometric data (age, sex, height).

Your data, your rights, your choices

At Xim, we want to make sure you find it easy to access and amend the data we hold about you. Subject to limitations, you can also make certain requests about that data. Please contact us using the details set out above if you wish to exercise your data rights, or contact the data protection regulator to find out more about them.

The right to be informed:

You have the right to be provided with clear, transparent and easily understandable information about how we use your information and your rights. This is why we’re providing you with the information in this Privacy Notice.

The right of access:

You have the right to obtain access to your information (if we’re processing it), and certain other information (similar to that provided in this Privacy Notice). This is so you’re aware and can check that we’re using your information in accordance with data protection law.

The right to rectification:

You are entitled to have your information corrected if it is inaccurate or incomplete though we may need to verify the accuracy of the new data you provide to us.

The right to erasure:

This is also known as ‘the right to be forgotten’ and, in simple terms, enables you to request the deletion or removal of your information that we hold and where there is no good reason for us continuing to process it. Note, however, that we may not always be able to comply with your request of erasure for specific legal reasons which will be notified to you, if applicable, at the time of your request.

The right to restrict processing:

You have rights to ‘block’ or suppress further use of your information. When processing is restricted, we can still store your information, but will not use it further except in limited circumstances.

The right to object to processing:

You have the right to object to certain types of processing, including processing for direct marketing (i.e. receiving information about Xim’s products and services which may be of interest to you via email or post) or where we are relying on a legitimate interest as the legal basis for processing. In some cases, we may demonstrate that we have compelling legitimate grounds to process your information which override your right to object although you have an absolute right to object where we are processing your data for direct marketing purposes.

The right to data portability:

You have the right to obtain and reuse your information for your own purposes across different services. To our best ability we will provide your information in an easily accessible format.

The right to lodge a complaint:

You have the right to lodge a complaint about the way we handle or process your information with the national data protection regulator.

The right to withdraw consent:

If you have given your consent to anything we do with your information (i.e. we rely on consent as a legal basis for processing your information), you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.

Please note that withdrawing your consent does not make unlawful what we have done with your personal data up to that point (when your consent was active).

If we receive a request from you to exercise any of the above rights, we may ask you to verify your identity before acting on the request; this is to ensure that your data is protected and kept secure. We may also contact you to ask you for further information in relation to your request to speed up our response.

Transfers outside the UK/EU

The information that we collect from you may be transferred to, processed and stored at destinations outside the UK and EEA. Where we transfer your data to countries outside of the UK and/or European Economic Area (‘EEA’), we will only do so if measures to maintain to protect your data and its privacy have been put in place.

Whenever we transfer your personal data out of the EEA and/or the UK, we ensure a similar degree of protection is afforded to it by ensuring at least one of the following safeguards is implemented:

  • we will only transfer your personal data to countries that have been deemed to provide an adequate level of protection for personal data by the European Commission (in the case of transfers out of the EEA) or the UK Government (in the case of transfers out of the UK); and/or
  • where we use certain service providers, we may use specific contracts approved by the European Commission (in the case of transfers out of the EEA) and/or the UK Government (in the case of transfers out of the UK), in both cases which give personal data the same protection it has within the EEA and/or UK as applicable. To obtain further information, please contact us.

Legitimate Interests Assessment

As noted in the ‘How we use your personal data (legal basis and purpose for processing)’ section of this Privacy Notice, we occasionally process your personal information under the legitimate interests’ legal basis. Where this is the case, we have carried out a thorough Legitimate Interests’ Assessment (‘LIA’) to ensure that we have weighed your interests and any risk posed to you against our own interests; ensuring that they are proportionate and appropriate.

How long we keep your data

Xim only ever retains personal information for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes we collected it for, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, regulatory, tax, accounting or reporting requirements and we have strict review and retention policies in place to meet these obligations. We are required under UK tax law to keep certain basic personal data of our customers for a minimum of 6 years after they cease being customers. As the device manufacturer of the Lifelight Application, we are required to keep certain personal data of patients and users of the Application for a minimum period of 10 years.

Where you have consented to us using your details for direct marketing, we will keep such data until you notify us otherwise and/or withdraw your consent.

National Data Opt-out - UK data subjects only

In the UK, the NHS, local authorities, university and hospital researchers, medical colleges and pharmaceutical companies researching new treatments can use your confidential patient information for research and planning. You can choose whether your confidential patient information is used for research and planning and you can change your choice at any time. If you do not want your confidential patient information to be used for research and planning, you can choose to opt out securely online or through a telephone service. Otherwise, you do not need to do anything.
